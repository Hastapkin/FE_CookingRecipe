PHỞ BÒ

Ingredients and Components
Rice noodles: White, tender, broad, and chewy, made from rice.

Beef broth: The star of the dish, simmered for hours from beef bones with spices like cinnamon, star anise, cloves, ginger, and dried onions. The broth is clear, naturally sweet, richly flavorful but not greasy.

Beef: Various cuts are used, including rare thinly sliced beef (cooked by dipping in hot broth), brisket, tendon, and flank to provide diverse textures and tastes.

Garnishes: Scallions, Thai basil, cilantro, bean sprouts, thinly sliced onions.

Additives: Lime, fresh chili, or chili sauce for extra sourness and spice.

Cooking Notes
The broth's clarity and depth of flavor come from long simmering with the proper balance of spices.

The variety of beef cuts adds richness and texture contrast within each bowl.

The fresh herbs and optional condiments allow diners to customize flavors to their preference.

Cultural Significance
Phở bò﻿ is not just a food but a cultural emblem representing Vietnamese pride, lifestyle, traditional culinary techniques, and creativity.
